Curious About Coffee?

Coffee is one of the world's most beloved beverages, known for its rich aroma and energizing effects. It originates from the seeds of the Coffea plant, and eventually becomes the liquid gold that fuels mornings around the globe. Here, you can learn more about the origins of coffee, the plants's preferred climate, and different types and roast options.



The Coffee Belt


The coffee belt is a tropical zone encircling the globe that encompasses parts of Central and South America, Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. This region provides the ideal climate for growing Coffea plants, with consistent rainfall, moderate temperatures, rich soil, and high humidity. Altitude within the coffee belt plays a crucial role in flavor development. The coffee belt isn’t just a geographic zone—it’s the heart of global coffee culture, sustaining millions of farmers and fueling a worldwide passion.

Types of Coffee Trees


The four main types of coffee plants—Arabica, Robusta, Liberica, and Excelsa. Each offer distinct flavors, growing conditions, and cultural significance. Arabica (Coffea arabica) is the most popular, accounting for about 60–70% of global coffee production. Grown at high altitudes in cooler climates, it produces smooth, aromatic beans with mild acidity and complex flavor profiles. Robusta (Coffea canephora) is hardier and grows at lower elevations. It contains more caffeine and has a stronger, more bitter taste, often used in espresso blends for its crema and punch.

Different Roasts


Coffee roasts differ in flavor, aroma, and body based on how long the beans are roasted. The three main categories are light, medium, and dark roasts.Light tend to have higher acidity and showcase subtle, often fruity or floral notes. Medium roasts offer a balanced profile with moderate acidity and a fuller body. The roasting process caramelizes sugars, producing a smoother taste with hints of chocolate or nuts. Dark roasts are roasted longer, resulting in bold, smoky flavors and low acidity. Choosing the right roast depends on your desired flavor experience.
