Cultivate Your Crafting Capabilities

Coffee is a versitile drink! It can be brewed and consumed in quite a few ways, and while there isn't necessarily a wrong way, there are a few best practices to observe to create a cup of competence. Understanding the roast, grind, and brew method for your beans can make the difference between a casual or choice cup of joe.



Explore Brew Styles

There are several brew methods, all with their own equipment, grind, water, and skill prerequisites. Usually, hot water is dispensed to soak ground coffee and is filtered by a metal or paper filter. Drip, pour-over, French press, and espresso brewing methods all employ this strategy to some degree. Iced coffee is normally brewed hot and to later be iced, but cold brew’s cycle uniquely begins and ends with cold water, keeping acidity minimal. However you choose to enjoy it, coffee’s a labor of love that requires time and care.

Mind the Grind

Grind size is pivotal in achieving an ideal flavor profile for any brewing method. Each type of brew has a specific texture to alter the way water interacts with the coffee taste. For example, pour-overs require a medium grind, which allows an even flow of water to moisten the grounds, producing balanced and rich notes. The size of the grounds also changes how long the coffee brews for. The finer the grind, the quicker the brew. Coarser-textured grinds need additional time to soak, and if the timing consistency is absent, the cup can become weak or sour.

Customize Your Cup

Coffee can be endlessly customized to personal taste. This beverage is enjoyed hot, iced, and blended to name a few. The taste can be sweetened by sugar, honey, or flavored syrups. Dairy or plant-based creamers can add richness and texture to the cup. Cinnamon, nutmeg, or cayenne elevates the flavor profile further. For an indulgent twist, whipped cream or sprinkles can spice it up. Coffee can be crafted to meet the dietary needs and seasonal cravings of just about anyone.
